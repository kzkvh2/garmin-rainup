# RainUp

> A Connect IQ data field for Garmin Edge cycling computers that tells you what the rain is doing **right now**, what's **coming up along the direction you're heading**, and **when it'll clear** — all in one glance from your handlebars.

**[Install on the Connect IQ Store](https://apps.garmin.com/)** *(link will go live once the app is approved)*

## What it does

Most weather fields show one forecast: where you are. **RainUp shows two** — what the rain is doing right now at your handlebars, and what to expect along the **direction you're actually heading**.

Forecasts at 30, 60, and 90 minutes ahead, dead-reckoned from your heading and pace. Refreshed every five minutes. One reading rule: top row is "now," rows below are "what's coming." A small icon on Row 3 tells you whether the forecast is **along your path (▲)** or **at your spot (⊙)** — so when you stop at a light and the message flips, you know why.

## Supported devices

Edge 530, 540, 550, 830, 840, 850, 1030 (base + Plus), 1040, 1050, Edge Explore 2, Edge MTB.

## Requires

A paired phone with Garmin Connect Mobile open in the foreground while you ride — RainUp uses the phone's bridge to fetch weather data.

## Privacy

RainUp collects nothing about you. The only data sent off-device is your GPS coordinates → Open-Meteo (the weather provider). No tracking, no analytics, no accounts. Full details: **[PRIVACY.md](https://github.com/kzkvh2/garmin-rainup/blob/main/PRIVACY.md)**.
